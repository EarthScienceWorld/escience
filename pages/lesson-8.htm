Lesson 8

MINERALS

Objectives

Identify common rock-forming minerals using their physical and chemical properties.

Discussion

A mineral is an inorganic, naturally occurring, crystalline substance represented by a specific chemical formula. Out of 2,000 known minerals only few make up most of the rocks in earth’s crust. Scientists put minerals into groups based on chemical composition. Carbonates are minerals that contain carbonate ion.

Carbonate minerals such as calcite and dolomite, are most common in sedimentary rocks. Halides are minerals that contain ions of the halogen elements bound to metal ions. Many halide minerals crystallize from shallow seas as the water evaporates. Native elements are minerals that contain atoms of only one element. Most native elements are metals, such as gold, copper and silver. Sulfates are minerals that contain the sulfate ion. Many sulfate minerals form when seawater evaporation. Silicates are minerals that contain compounds of silicon and oxygen. Silicate minerals make up most of the rock and earth.

Scientists identify minerals based on their properties of hardness, luster, streak, cleavage and fracture. The hardness of a mineral describes how resistant the mineral is to being scratched. The bonds between the atoms in a mineral affect the way the mineral breaks. Some minerals, such as muscovite, break along distinct planes to form sides. Minerals that break in this way have cleavage. A break that does not form a flat plane is known as fracture. The luster of a mineral is a description of the way the mineral reflects light. Galena, for example, reflects light the way shiny metals do. Scientists determine the streak color of a mineral by rubbing the mineral on a streak plate. The streak color may be very different from the color of the mineral itself and is the same for all samples of the mineral. For example, the mineral hematite may be black, gray or orange in color, but its streak is always dark red.

Trivia

Task

Assessment

  1. Which of the following terms is NOT used to describe a mineral’s luster?

    2. Glassy
    3. Dull
    4. Hexagonal
  2. The color of the powder that a mineral leaves on a piece of white, unglazed porcelain is called the mineral’s
    1. Hardness
    2. Luster
    3. Streak
    4. Scratch
  3. The most common classification of minerals is based on
    1. Color
    2. Chemical Composition
    3. Size
    4. Shape
  4. Which of the following is considered a special property that applies to only a few minerals?
    1. Color
    2. Luster
    3. Streak
    4. Magnetism
